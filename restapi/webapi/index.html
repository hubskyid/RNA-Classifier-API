About RNA Classification

What is RNA Classification?

RNA classification is the process of identifying and categorizing RNA molecules based on their sequence and structural features. This tool analyzes both the primary sequence (nucleotide composition) and secondary structure (folding patterns) to classify RNA into functional families and identify similar known structures.

Secondary Structure Analysis

RNA secondary structure represents the folding pattern of the RNA molecule, showing which bases pair with each other. We use dot-bracket notation where:

  • Dots (.) represent unpaired nucleotides
  • Parentheses () represent base pairs, with matching opening and closing brackets

How to Use This Tool

  1. Enter RNA Sequence: Input your RNA sequence using standard nucleotide notation (A, U, G, C)
  2. Add Structure Information: Provide the secondary structure in dot-bracket format
  3. Submit for Analysis: Click the classify button to process your data
  4. Review Results: Examine classification results and similarity matches
  5. Export Data: Download results for further analysis or record keeping

Applications

This tool has broad applications in RNA research and biomedical science. By classifying RNA sequences and identifying structural similarities, it enables deeper exploration of RNA function, structural overlap across families, and evolutionary relationships. Such insights are essential for advancing areas like vaccine development, therapeutic RNA design, and drug discovery. The ability to accurately retrieve and classify RNA sequences also supports more reliable structure prediction, aiding both fundamental research and translational medicine.
